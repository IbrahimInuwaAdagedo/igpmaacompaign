About IGP Mohammad Abubakar Adamu

Mohammed Abubakar Adamu is a Nigerian law-enforcement professional and former Inspector General of Police (IGP). Born on 17 September 1961 in Lafia, Nasarawa State, he attended Government Teachers College, Wamba, and later studied Geography and Education at Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, graduating in 1984.

Adamu began his policing career in 1986 with the Nigeria Police Force, rising through various operational and administrative ranks in Nigeria and in international service with INTERPOL. He served as the 20th Inspector General of Police of Nigeria from January 2019 to April 2021, overseeing national law-enforcement operations, police reforms, and inter-agency security collaboration. After retirement, he continues to engage in public service and community development initiatives in Nasarawa State.

Early Life and Education

Adamu was born in Lafia, Nasarawa State, where he completed his early education at Government Teachers College, Wamba. He earned his Bachelor’s degree in Geography and Education from Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, in 1984 and later pursued professional courses in law enforcement and security administration both in Nigeria and overseas.

Police Career

Adamu joined the Nigeria Police Force in 1986 as a Cadet Assistant Superintendent of Police. He served in various commands and departments, gaining experience in criminal investigation, administration, and international policing.

Between 1997 and 2010, he was seconded to INTERPOL in Lyon, France, where he worked in the Economic and Financial Crimes Unit and later rose to become Assistant Director in charge of African Affairs, coordinating cross-border crime-fighting initiatives among African member states.

Leadership as Inspector General of Police

On 15 January 2019, Adamu was appointed as the 20th Inspector General of Police of Nigeria. His tenure emphasized community policing, modernization of police operations, and strengthened collaboration with regional and international security agencies.

He introduced the Community Policing Initiative and led reforms aimed at improving professionalism, accountability, and public trust in the Nigeria Police Force.

Post-Service Activities

Since leaving active police service, Adamu has participated in public lectures, youth mentorship programs, and security advisory roles. He remains active in civic engagements within Nasarawa State, contributing to discussions on public safety, governance, and community development.
